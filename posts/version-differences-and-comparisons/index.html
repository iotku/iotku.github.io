Version differences and comparisons

Posted: 2013-12-05 10:20   |  More posts about speedrunning

A lot of fuss has been go­ing on re­cent­ly (and on­go­ing for quite some time) about ver­sion dif­fer­ences be­tween games such as Oca­ri­na of Time and Su­per Mario 64 main­ly (or at least the most vo­cal­ly). On the Oca­ri­na of Time side, Cos­mo got a fair bit of flak about pre­vi­ous­ly (has since been de­feat­ed) gain­ing the Any% ~World Record~ on an "ob­scure" Chi­nese re­lease of the N64 (with dif­fer­ences that make it faster than both N64 and VC), the iQue, in­stead of the gen­er­al­ly ac­cept­ed Vir­tu­al Con­sole re­lease. While on the Su­per Mario 64 side, the main fight is be­tween N64 and VC which re­moves near­ly all lag, which as a re­sult a lot of chal­lenge in lag re­duc­tion and saves a bunch of ex­tra time.

I don't have as big of an in­ter­est in the Oca­ri­na of Time side of this be­cause I don't run the game, how­ev­er it gives me per­spec­tive on the oth­er side of the fence. In the OOT camp N64 is pret­ty ac­cept­able (gen­er­al­ly be­cause a glitch ex­clu­sive to 1.0 ver­sion which was on­ly re­leased on the N64 I be­lieve), but so is VC due to it be­ing a "Faster Of­fi­cial Re­lease" (less lag main­ly). So at this point the com­mu­ni­ty has al­ready es­tab­lished that they on­ly care about "This game runs the fastest and it's an of­fi­cial re­lease", that seems to be the main cri­te­ri­a. Giv­en what I in­ter­pret the OOT com­mu­ni­ty to care about on­ly be­ing the fastest I was pret­ty shocked to see the out­rage over the iQue. Sure it's not easy to get a hold of, cheap, etc; nei­ther is a 1.0 N64 cart from my un­der­stand­ing. Do you have to get a "World Record"? No. You could buy an iQue in or­der to save 13~ sec­ond­s, or you could just con­tin­ue play­ing on your ver­sion re­gard­less and stop wor­ry­ing about some "Ob­scure Chi­nese con­sole". You can still be a com­pet­i­tive play­er re­gard­less of be­ing on a slight­ly slow­er plat­for­m, you just get less free time.

As for Su­per Mario 64, I have a bit more in­ter­est in­to the fight be­tween N64 and VC as I run the game my­self on the "s­low­er" plat­form N64. A lot of the "big­ger names" in the SM64 speedrun­ning com­mu­ni­ty de­test VC; the gen­er­al rea­son seems to be around the lines of it be­ing "less skill­ful". The es­ti­mate that gets thrown around (if I re­mem­ber cor­rect­ly) off the top of my head is that VC saves 1:30 in a 120 star run vs N64 with good lag re­duc­tion strat­s. That's cer­tain­ly more than 13~ sec­ond­s, a big amount of time in a very tech­ni­cal game. You al­so don't need to put in any ef­fort in­to lag re­duc­tion, mak­ing VC less of a chal­lenge in some cas­es, as you don't have to fight with the cam­era as much. Even­tu­al­ly af­ter some fight­ing among pret­ty much ev­ery­one the Ja­pa­nese leader­board sep­a­rat­ed VC from N64 (and oth­ers fol­lowed along) much of the prob­lem got set­tled. While peo­ple still ar­gue about the ver­sion, many less ar­gue about the com­par­i­son.

While nei­ther of these cas­es are ex­act­ly clear cut, they're all tech­ni­cal­ly Nin­ten­do re­leased ver­sions of the same games, I feel that the main is­sue is com­par­ing ap­ples to or­anges. Why should we di­rect­ly com­pare dif­fer­ent ver­sions that have big enough dif­fer­ences that peo­ple fight al­most ev­ery day over them? The sim­pler so­lu­tion is to just sep­a­rate ev­ery­thing and if any­one cares enough they can com­pare on their own (or with au­to­mat­ed help). Sure it would be nice to know the best times for each ver­sion, but that does­n't mean ev­ery­thing should be com­bined in the first place. If some­body wants to com­pare the best VC time with iQue times they can check it on their own. If some­one wants to com­pare PAL sm64 to Japane­se/Amer­i­can NTSC they can do so al­so. It's not re­al­ly that dif­fi­cult to do, even on a sep­a­rat­ed leader­board.
